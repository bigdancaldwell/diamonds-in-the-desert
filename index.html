Why it matters

Every entry fee builds a real career.

Now in its 6th year, Diamonds in the Desert is planned, run, and hosted by the students of the CDO Autos program at Canyon del Oro High School — with student participants from across the Amphi district.

It's more than a show. It's a working classroom, where students gain real skills in automotive technology, business management, customer service, and event planning that carry straight into apprenticeships and the shop floor.

"100% of every dollar raised goes straight back into the tools, parts, and training that put our students to work."
100% of proceeds stay with the CDO automotive program — tax-deductible through CDO Auto Boosters.

A student-powered event

They don't just show up. They run it.

From the first planning meeting to the final trophy, this show is powered by CDO students — and every role is a real-world skill in the making.

01

They plan it

Students handle logistics, layout, registration, and outreach — learning project planning and organization from the ground up.

02

They build it

The automotive program is where students develop real technical expertise — diagnostics, repair, and the craft behind the cars.

03

They run it

On show day, students manage the field, greet exhibitors, and handle customer service — hospitality and leadership in action.
